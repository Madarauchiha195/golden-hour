GOLDEN HOUR MEDICAL COORDINATOR - EMERGENCY TRIAGE PROTOCOLS

=== SECTION 1: TRIAGE CATEGORY CLASSIFICATION ===

CATEGORY: CARDIOLOGY
Symptoms: Chest pain, chest tightness, shortness of breath with chest pain, radiating pain to left arm/jaw/back, palpitations, syncope with chest pain, diaphoresis, nausea with chest pain
Severity Indicators:
- CRITICAL: ST-elevation MI symptoms, cardiac arrest, unstable angina, acute heart failure, cardiogenic shock, ventricular fibrillation
- HIGH: Non-ST elevation MI, new onset atrial fibrillation with rapid ventricular response, hypertensive emergency (BP > 180/120), acute pericarditis with tamponade
- MEDIUM: Stable angina, controlled atrial fibrillation, mild heart failure exacerbation, hypertensive urgency
- LOW: Palpitations without hemodynamic compromise, benign chest wall pain, stable chronic heart conditions

CATEGORY: NEUROLOGY
Symptoms: Sudden severe headache, focal weakness, speech difficulty, facial droop, vision changes, seizures, altered mental status, numbness/tingling one side, confusion, loss of consciousness
Severity Indicators:
- CRITICAL: Acute stroke symptoms (within 4.5 hours), status epilepticus, intracranial hemorrhage, Glasgow Coma Scale < 8, spinal cord compression, brain herniation
- HIGH: TIA (transient ischemic attack), new onset seizures, meningitis symptoms (fever + neck stiffness + headache), encephalitis, acute hydrocephalus
- MEDIUM: Complex migraine, post-ictal state, peripheral neuropathy with acute weakness, vertigo with neurological signs
- LOW: Chronic headache exacerbation, peripheral neuropathy without acute changes, simple vertigo

CATEGORY: TRAUMA
Symptoms: Blunt or penetrating injury, fall from height, motor vehicle accident, crush injury, burns, amputation, open fractures, head injury
Severity Indicators:
- CRITICAL: Penetrating trauma to chest/abdomen/head, hemodynamic instability from trauma, GCS < 13, open skull fracture, tension pneumothorax, massive hemorrhage, traumatic cardiac arrest, flail chest, pelvic fracture with instability
- HIGH: Significant mechanism of injury (fall > 20 feet, high-speed MVC, ejection), multiple long bone fractures, spinal injury with deficit, significant burns (>20% BSA or face/airway), compartment syndrome
- MEDIUM: Isolated long bone fracture, moderate burns (10-20% BSA), lacerations requiring repair, concussion with brief LOC
- LOW: Minor lacerations, sprains, contusions, minor burns (<10% BSA), isolated digit injuries

CATEGORY: RESPIRATORY
Symptoms: Severe shortness of breath, wheezing, stridor, cough with hemoptysis, respiratory distress, oxygen desaturation, choking, inability to speak full sentences
Severity Indicators:
- CRITICAL: Respiratory arrest, severe anaphylaxis with airway compromise, tension pneumothorax, massive hemoptysis, acute respiratory failure (SpO2 < 85% on room air), complete airway obstruction, status asthmaticus not responding to treatment
- HIGH: Severe asthma exacerbation, COPD exacerbation with respiratory distress, pulmonary embolism, spontaneous pneumothorax, moderate anaphylaxis, epiglottitis, severe pneumonia with hypoxia
- MEDIUM: Moderate asthma exacerbation responding to treatment, community-acquired pneumonia, pleural effusion, mild COPD exacerbation
- LOW: Upper respiratory infection, mild asthma, chronic cough evaluation, mild bronchitis

CATEGORY: GENERAL EMERGENCY
Symptoms: Severe abdominal pain, allergic reactions, poisoning/overdose, diabetic emergencies, sepsis, acute renal failure, GI bleeding, psychiatric emergency
Severity Indicators:
- CRITICAL: Septic shock, anaphylactic shock, acute abdomen with hemodynamic instability, massive GI hemorrhage, diabetic ketoacidosis with altered consciousness, severe poisoning with organ failure
- HIGH: Severe sepsis, acute appendicitis, ectopic pregnancy, diabetic ketoacidosis, significant GI bleeding, acute pancreatitis, bowel obstruction
- MEDIUM: Moderate abdominal pain with stable vitals, controlled diabetic emergency, mild allergic reaction, urinary retention
- LOW: Mild abdominal complaints, minor allergic reactions, stable chronic conditions

=== SECTION 2: AGE-BASED RISK MODIFIERS ===

Pediatric (0-14 years):
- Increase severity by one level for respiratory and neurological presentations
- Lower thresholds for vital sign abnormalities
- Pediatric GCS modifications apply

Elderly (65+ years):
- Increase severity by one level for trauma (due to frailty, anticoagulation)
- Lower threshold for sepsis (may not mount fever)
- Consider atypical presentations (MI without chest pain, stroke without focal deficit)
- Falls in elderly: always evaluate for head injury even if mechanism seems minor

=== SECTION 3: VITAL SIGN RED FLAGS ===

Heart Rate: <40 or >150 bpm = CRITICAL; 40-50 or 130-150 = HIGH
Blood Pressure: Systolic <80 or >200 = CRITICAL; 80-90 or 180-200 = HIGH
Respiratory Rate: <8 or >35 = CRITICAL; 8-10 or 28-35 = HIGH
SpO2: <85% = CRITICAL; 85-90% = HIGH; 90-94% = MEDIUM
Temperature: <35°C or >40°C = HIGH; 35-36°C or 38.5-40°C = MEDIUM
GCS: <8 = CRITICAL; 8-12 = HIGH; 13-14 = MEDIUM
